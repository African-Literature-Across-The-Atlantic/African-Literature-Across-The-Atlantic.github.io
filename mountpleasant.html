Novel : Mount Pleasant


In Mont Pleasant, nine-year-old Sara is taken from her mother in 1931 and sent to Sultan Njoya’s court in Yaoundé, where she is disguised as a boy named Nebu to protect her. As World War II looms, Sara grows up in a royal court rich in art and culture, led by the visionary Sultan Njoya. Seventy years later, she shares her remarkable story with Bertha, a young Cameroonian student. The novel weaves personal and political histories, exploring love, colonialism, and the fading glory of the Bamoun civilization.

Author : Patrice Nganang


Patrice Nganang is a Cameroonian writer, author of more than fifteen books including the novel Dog Days for which he won the Marguerite Yourcenar Literary Award and the Black African Literary Grand Prize. He is Head of the Department of African, African American and Caribbean Studies (Africana) at the State University of New York, Stony Brook where he teaches literary and cultural theory.
