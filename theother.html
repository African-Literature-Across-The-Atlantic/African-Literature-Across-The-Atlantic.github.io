Novel : The Other Americans


Late one spring night, Driss Guerraoui, a Moroccan immigrant in California, is killed by a speeding car. The repercussions of his death bring together a diverse cast of characters, from his daughter, a jazz composer, to a veteran from the Iraq war, or an undocumented witness whose fear of deportation prevents him from coming forward. As the characters tell their stories, the invisible connections that tie them together–even while they remain deeply divided by race, religion, or class–are slowly revealed. When the mystery of what happened to Driss Guerraoui unfolds, a town’s hypocrisies are faced, and love is born.

Author : Laila Lalami


Laila Lalami, born in Rabat and educated in Morocco, the UK, and the U.S., is the author of five acclaimed books. Her novel The Moor’s Account won multiple awards and was a finalist for the Pulitzer Prize and longlisted for the Booker Prize. Her latest novel, The Other Americans, was a national bestseller and a finalist for the National Book Award. A distinguished professor of creative writing at UC Riverside, Lalami’s essays appear in major outlets like The New York Times and The Guardian. She lives in Los Angeles.
