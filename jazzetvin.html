Novel : Jazz et Vin de Palme


In Jazz and Palm Wine, Emmanuel Dongala blends jazz, aliens, and witchcraft in a vibrant collection of stories rooted in Kongo culture. The first half explores post-colonial Congo, where traditional beliefs clash with state-imposed rationalism amid corruption and hardship. The second half shifts to 1960s America, where civil rights struggles meet surreal twistsâ€”like leaders using jazz and palm wine to thwart aliens. Through these imaginative tales, Dongala tackles identity, race, and coexistence, suggesting that art offers a path to harmony and freedom.

Author : Emmanuel B. Dongala


Emmanuel Dongala is a Congolese American chemist and author of the award-winning novels Johnny Mad Dog (2002) and The Bridgetower Sonata (2017). He obtained his BA and MS in Chemistry in the United States, at Oberlin College and Rutgers University respectively. He went on to pursue his PhD in France at Montpellier University. Dongola received the Black African Literature Award in 1988.
