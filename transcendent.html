Novel : Transcendent Kingdom


Yaa Gyasi’s powerful follow-up to Homegoing is a deeply layered novel centered on a Ghanaian family living in Alabama. Gifty is a sixth-year neuroscience PhD candidate at Stanford, researching the brain circuits behind depression and addiction. Her determination is fueled by the tragic loss of her brother Nana, a talented athlete who died of a heroin overdose after becoming addicted to OxyContin following an injury. Meanwhile, her mother struggles with suicidal despair. As Gifty seeks scientific explanations for her family’s suffering, she also grapples with her childhood faith and the evangelical church where she was raised—drawn to its promise of salvation even as it feels distant and uncertain.


Author : Yaa Gyasi


Yaa Gyasi is a Ghanaian-American novelist raised in Huntsville, Alabama. She is the author of the novel Homegoing (2016) which won the National Book Critics Circle’s John Leonard Award for best first book. She holds a BA in English from Stanford University and an MFA from the Iowa Writers’ Workshop, where she held a Dean’s Graduate Research Fellowship. Currently, she lives in Brooklyn.
