Novel : Pelourinho


Pelourinho by Tierno Monénembo is often called the most Brazilian of African novels. Set in Salvador de Bahia, once home to Brazil’s oldest slave market, it follows an African writer—nicknamed "Escritore" or "Africano" by his favela friends—on a journey to uncover his ancestral roots. Seen by locals as the prophesied Prince of Dahomey, his visit becomes a spiritual and personal quest to trace the history of his people, taken from Africa to work the plantations of the New World. The novel explores identity, memory, and the enduring ties between Africa and its diaspora.

Author : Tierno Monénembo


Tierno Monénembo, winner of the 2008 Renaudot Prize, is a Guinean novelist known for his powerful explorations of exile and identity. Born in 1947 and exiled at 23, he has published thirteen novels that span continents—from West Africa to France, Brazil, and beyond. His fiction, rich with nostalgia and cultural insight, creates a unique literary universe rooted in personal and collective journeys. In Pelourinho, his fifth novel, Monénembo follows an African writer’s search for meaning in Brazil, reflecting his broader themes of displacement and belonging.
