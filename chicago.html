Novel : Chicago


Egypt is here, in the heart of an America traumatized by the terrorist attacks of September 11th. As the Egyptian president's official visit to Chicago is announced, the embassy's police system is set in motion, orchestrated by the redoubtable Safouat Chaker, who controls and monitors all Egyptians living in the United States of America. From one chapter to the next, the author interweaves lives that are searching for each other and getting lost in the meanders of the contemporary world, lives scarred by having been transplanted into a universe that is both strange and foreign, no matter how much they may wish to identify with the American way of life.

Author : Alaa Al-Aswany


Alaa al-Aswany, Egyptian author known for his best-selling novels and for his vocal criticism of the Egyptian government, especially its former president Hosni Mubarak. Aswany was the son of Abbas al-Aswany, a lawyer enamoured of literature who was credited with reviving the maqāmah (anecdotes written in rhymed prose) genre and who won the 1972 State Award for Literature for his novel Al-Aswār al-ʿāliyah (“High Walls”). The younger Aswany attended the French Lycée in Cairo and graduated with an undergraduate degree (1980) in dentistry from Cairo University. He received an M.S. in dentistry from the University of Illinois at Chicago, finishing in only 11 months. Source: Britannica
