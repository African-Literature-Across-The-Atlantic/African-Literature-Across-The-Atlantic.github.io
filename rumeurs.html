Novel : Rumeurs d'Amérique


Here, Alain Mabanckou has blended into the crowd; he has felt the pulse of those who share his skin color and those who are different from him, with whom he interacts daily. Some places whisper their history to him because he knows them intimately. Others resist him, and sometimes he has to dig long to finally see their true face emerge. Between the twists of the unusual, the digression of the anecdote, and the mirages of the imagination, the writer delivers an American autobiography that is infinitely personal. Alain Mabanckou is a poet and novelist. In 2015, he was a Professor of Artistic Creation at the Collège de France. He currently teaches French literature at UCLA. He is notably the author of 'Memoirs of a Porcupine'.

Author : Alain Mabanckou


Alain Mabanckou, born in the Republic of the Congo, is a novelist, journalist, poet and academic. He is the author of African Psycho (2003) and the acclaimed Verre Cassé (2005). Mabanckou is the first francophone sub-Saharan African writer to have been published by Gallimard in its prestigious "collection Blanche". He is currently a professor of French and Francophone studies at the University of California, Los Angeles.
