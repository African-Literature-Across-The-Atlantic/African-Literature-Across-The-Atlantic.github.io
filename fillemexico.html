Novel : Filles de Mexico


In Mexico, Djibril Nawo, a French writer of Togolese origin, intrigues. He elicits contempt from some, fascination and desire from others. From the slums to the upscale neighborhoods, Djibril wanders the city encountering a multitude of characters—unsettling, endearing, or simply strange. His encounter with Deliz Gamboa will be crucial: the charm of this Colombian poetess has nothing to do with that of Mexican prostitutes... From realism to phantasmagoria, from Mexico to Colombia, Sami Tchak leads us towards distant horizons. In the tone of tragicomedy, "Filles de Mexico" evokes issues of social and racial inequalities, violence, and sexuality, inseparably intertwined.

Author : Sami Tchak


Sami Tchak is a Togolese born writer of the novels Femme infidèle (1988) and Place des Fetes (2001). Having obtained his PhD at Sorbonne University, his research on prostitution in Cuba led him to the island resulting in the publication of the essay “Prostitution a Cuba. Communisme, ruses and debrouilles”. Since 2003, his works has been mostly focusing on Latin America. In 2004, Tchak won the Grand Prix of Black African Literature for the entire range of his work.
