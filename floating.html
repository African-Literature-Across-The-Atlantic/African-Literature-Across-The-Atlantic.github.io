Novel : Floating in a Most Peculiar Way


A brilliant, displaced boy journeys from Biafra to Jamaica to the streets of Los Angeles in this powerful memoir. In a strict Jamaican household for abandoned children, Chude-Sokei learns he is the “first son of the first son” of a famed Biafran leader—marking the start of a complex search for identity. A visiting African falls to his knees, shaking the boy in disbelief: “Is this him?” Chude-Sokei’s journey through the politics of race and diaspora shifts when his mother—once the “Jackie O of Biafra”—calls him to join her in Inglewood, Los Angeles. Amid gangsta rap and rising unrest, his love of sci-fi and David Bowie marks him as alien. Struggling to become a Black American, he finds guidance in a loud, loving pan-African network that teaches him to navigate the many meanings of Blackness in America.


Author : Louis Chude-Sokei


Louis Chude-Sokei is a Nigerian born scholar and writer whose work deals with the African Diaspora. He is the Editor-in-Chief of “The Black Scholar” and writer of the essay “Dr. Satan’s Echo Chamber”. He is also currently the Director of African American Studies at Boston University. His books include the award-winning, The Last Darky: Bert Williams, Black on Black Minstrelsy, and the African Diaspora (2005), The Sound of Culture: Diaspora and Black Technopoetics (2015) and the acclaimed memoir, Floating in A Most Peculiar Way (2021).
